Delen door een breuk is hetzelfde als vermenigvuldigen met het omgekeerde.

We schrijven dus eerst \color{#FFA500}{WHOLE} en \color{#FFA500}{WHOLE2} als breuken:

\color{#FFA500}{WHOLE} = \color{#6495ED}{fraction(WHOLE,1)} = \color{#6495ED}{fraction(WHOLE*REDUCE[1],REDUCE[1])}

\color{#FFA500}{WHOLE2} = \color{#6495ED}{fraction(WHOLE2,1)} = \color{#6495ED}{fraction(WHOLE2*REDUCED[1],REDUCED[1])}

Nu tellen we \color{#FFA500}{WHOLE} en \color{#6495ED}{fraction(REDUCE[0],REDUCE[1])} bij elkaar op:

\color{#6495ED}{fraction(WHOLE*REDUCE[1],REDUCE[1])} + \color{#6495ED}{fraction(REDUCE[0],REDUCE[1])} = \color{#6495ED}{fraction(WHOLE*REDUCE[1]+REDUCE[0],REDUCE[1])}

Nu tellen we \color{#FFA500}{WHOLE2} en \color{#6495ED}{fraction(REDUCED[0],REDUCED[1])} bij elkaar op:

\color{#6495ED}{fraction(WHOLE2*REDUCED[1],REDUCED[1])} + \color{#6495ED}{fraction(REDUCED[0],REDUCED[1])} = \color{#6495ED}{fraction(WHOLE2*REDUCED[1]+REDUCED[0],REDUCED[1])}

Nu kunnen we de twee breuken door elkaar delen:

\displaystyle {} \color{#6495ED}{fraction(WHOLE*REDUCE[1]+REDUCE[0],REDUCE[1])} \div \color{#6495ED}{fraction(REDUCED[1]*WHOLE2+REDUCED[0],REDUCED[1])}

Nu moeten we de breuk nog vereenvoudigen:

\color{#6495ED}{fraction(WHOLE*REDUCE[1]*REDUCE[1], REDUCE[1]*(REDUCE[0]+REDUCE[1]*WHOLE2))} = \color{#6495ED}{fraction(REDUCE2[0],REDUCE2[1])}
