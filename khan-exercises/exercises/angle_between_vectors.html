To calculate the cosine of the angle between two vectors, we use the formula \cos(\theta) = \frac{\vec x \cdot \vec y}{||\vec x|| \hspace{1 pt} ||\vec y||}

First, we calculate the dot product of \color{#6495ED}{\vec a} and \color{#28AE7B}{\vec b}:
\sum\limits_{i=0}^{n} \color{#6495ED}{\vec a}_i \times \color{#28AE7B}{\vec b}_i where n = vector length.

For \color{#6495ED}{\vec a} and \color{#28AE7B}{\vec b}, that is showDot(VA,VB)

That is showHalfdot(VA,VB)

= DOT

Then, we calculate the lengths of \vec a and \vec b by taking the square root of the dot product of the vector with itself.

\color{#6495ED}{\vec a}: \sqrt{showDot(VA,VA)}
\color{#28AE7B}{\vec b}: \sqrt{showDot(VB,VB)}

\color{#6495ED}{\vec a}: \sqrt{showHalfdot(VA,VA)}
\color{#28AE7B}{\vec b}: \sqrt{showHalfdot(VB,VB)}

\color{#6495ED}{\vec a} = \sqrt{DOT1}
\color{#28AE7B}{\vec b} = \sqrt{DOT2}

Multiplying \sqrt{DOT1} and \sqrt{DOT2}, we get \sqrt{DOT1*DOT2}.

Now, divide DOT by \sqrt{DOT1*DOT2}

\frac{DOT}{\sqrt{DOT1*DOT2}} = DOT/Math.sqrt(DOT1*DOT2)
